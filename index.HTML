The Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, is one of the most iconic symbols of India. It is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a masterpiece of Mughal architecture. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, the Taj Mahal is known for its stunning white marble dome and beautiful gardens.

The Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar is a towering minaret located in Delhi, standing at 73 meters tall. Built in the early 13th century by Qutb-ud-Din Aibak, it is the tallest brick minaret in the world. It is another UNESCO World Heritage site and an important example of Indo-Islamic Afghan architecture.

Ellora Caves

The Ellora Caves are a complex of rock-cut monasteries and temples located in Maharashtra. They date back to between the 5th and 10th centuries. The caves are known for their stunning carvings and sculptures, especially the Kailasa temple, which is a single monolithic structure carved out of a hill.
