Overview

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is one of the most famous landmarks in the world. A symbol of love, it is a stunning example of Mughal architecture and has been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site. Known for its grandeur, the Taj Mahal is visited by millions of tourists each year and is often referred to as the "Crown of Palaces."

History

The Taj Mahal was built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died during childbirth in 1631. Construction began in 1632 and was completed in 1653, taking over two decades to complete. It is made of white marble and set in expansive gardens, with intricate carvings and inlay work. The Taj Mahal is also a symbol of the Mughal Empire's wealth and artistic vision.

Architecture

The Taj Mahal blends Persian, Ottoman Turkish, Mughal, and Indian architectural styles. Its central structure is an enormous white marble dome, surrounded by lush gardens, reflecting pools, and various outbuildings. The dome, which is 35 meters high, is surrounded by four minarets that provide a sense of balance and symmetry. The entire structure is adorned with intricate carvings, floral designs, and precious gemstones, making it a remarkable feat of engineering and design.
